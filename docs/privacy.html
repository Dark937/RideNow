Data Controller

Ride S.r.l. ("Ride", "we", "us") is the data controller for the personal data collected through our platform, application, and services. We are subject to the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Italian Legislative Decree 196/2003 (as amended by D.Lgs. 101/2018).

VAT number: IT12345678901
DPO contact: privacy@ride.app
02

Data We Collect

We collect personal data in the following categories:

Account & Identity

  • Full name, email address, phone number
  • Profile photo (if provided)
  • Date of birth (for age verification)
  • City and country of residence

Ride & Location Data

  • Precise GPS location during ride booking and while a ride is active
  • Pickup and drop-off addresses
  • Trip history, duration, distance, and route taken
  • Approximate location inferred from IP address when the app is in background

Payment Data

  • Payment method type (card, Apple Pay, Google Pay)
  • Last 4 digits of card and expiry date (full card data is held by our payment processor, never Ride)
  • Billing address and transaction history

Device & Usage Data

  • Device model, operating system, and app version
  • IP address and browser type
  • Crash logs and performance metrics
  • Features used and session duration

Communications

  • In-app messages and support tickets
  • Ratings and reviews you submit
  • Survey responses and feedback
03

How We Use Your Data

We use your personal data to:

  • Provide, operate, and improve our ride-hailing platform
  • Match you with available drivers in real time
  • Process payments and send transaction receipts
  • Send service notifications (driver arrival, trip updates, receipts)
  • Provide customer support and resolve disputes
  • Detect, investigate, and prevent fraud, abuse, and safety incidents
  • Comply with legal obligations (tax records, regulatory reporting)
  • Conduct analytics to improve service quality and safety
  • Send promotional communications (only with your consent)
  • Personalise your experience through the Fidelity programme
04

Legal Basis for Processing

Under the GDPR, we rely on the following legal bases:

  • Contract performance — processing necessary to provide the ride service you have requested (Art. 6(1)(b))
  • Legal obligation — processing required by applicable law, such as tax and anti-money-laundering regulations (Art. 6(1)(c))
  • Legitimate interests — fraud prevention, security, network analytics, and improving service quality (Art. 6(1)(f))
  • Consent — marketing communications, precise background location, and optional analytics sharing (Art. 6(1)(a)). You may withdraw consent at any time from Settings → Privacy.
05

Sharing Your Data

We do not sell your personal data. We may share data with:

  • Drivers — your first name, pickup location, and destination during a booked ride. Precise GPS and contact details are limited and not retained by drivers after trip completion.
  • Payment processors — Stripe and Apple Pay/Google Pay receive payment data necessary to complete transactions. They operate under their own privacy policies and are PCI-DSS compliant.
  • Cloud & infrastructure providers — AWS (EU region) for hosting; all data is stored within the EEA.
  • Analytics partners — anonymised, aggregated usage data only, where you have consented.
  • Law enforcement & regulatory bodies — when required by law, court order, or to protect the safety of users and the public.
  • Business transfers — in the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your data may be transferred to the successor entity. We will notify you before this occurs.
06

Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes described in this Notice, unless a longer retention period is required by law.

  • Account data — retained for the life of your account, plus 30 days after deletion (to allow recovery)
  • Trip history — retained for 7 years for tax and regulatory compliance
  • Payment records — retained for 10 years under Italian fiscal law
  • Support communications — retained for 3 years after resolution
  • Location data — precise GPS retained for 90 days; aggregated route data retained for up to 2 years
  • Marketing consent records — retained for 3 years from the date of consent
07

International Transfers

Your personal data is primarily stored and processed within the European Economic Area (EEA). Where we transfer data outside the EEA (for example, to third-party service providers), we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, including:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission
  • Adequacy decisions issued by the European Commission
  • Binding Corporate Rules where applicable
EU–US Data Transfers: Where data is transferred to the United States, we rely on Standard Contractual Clauses and, where applicable, the EU–US Data Privacy Framework.
08

Your Rights

Under the GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access — request a copy of the data we hold about you
  • Rectification — ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Erasure — request deletion of your data ("right to be forgotten"), subject to legal retention requirements
  • Restriction — ask us to limit how we use your data in certain circumstances
  • Portability — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Objection — object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing
  • Withdraw consent — at any time, for processing based on consent
  • Lodge a complaint — with the Italian Data Protection Authority (Garante) at garanteprivacy.it

You can exercise most of these rights directly from Settings → Privacy → Your Data. For requests that cannot be fulfilled in-app, contact us at privacy@ride.app. We will respond within 30 days.

09

Cookies & Tracking

Our website and application use cookies and similar tracking technologies for the following purposes:

  • Essential cookies — required for the platform to function (authentication, session management). These cannot be disabled.
  • Performance cookies — collect anonymous data on how users interact with the platform to help us improve it.
  • Analytics cookies — used to understand aggregate usage patterns. Only set with your consent.
  • Marketing cookies — used to measure the effectiveness of campaigns. Only set with your consent.

You can manage your cookie preferences at any time from Settings → Privacy. Withdrawing consent for non-essential cookies does not affect the functionality of the core ride service.

10

Children's Privacy

Our Services are not directed at individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us at privacy@ride.app and we will take steps to delete it promptly.

Parents & guardians: If you discover that a minor has created a Ride account without consent, please contact our support team immediately for assistance.
11

Security

We implement industry-standard technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data, including:

  • TLS/SSL encryption for all data in transit
  • AES-256 encryption for data at rest
  • Multi-factor authentication for internal systems access
  • Regular third-party security audits and penetration testing
  • Strict role-based access controls and data minimisation policies
  • Automated monitoring for anomalous data access patterns

In the event of a data breach that is likely to result in high risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify you and the relevant supervisory authority as required by Article 33 and 34 of the GDPR.
